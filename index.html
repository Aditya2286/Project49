Camel

Interesting Facts About Camels

1) Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!
2) Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.
3) Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.

Cicada

Interesting Facts About Cicada

1) Cicadas are about the size of a grain of rice when they drop from a tree branch to the ground and start digging.
2) Cicadas don’t sing at night, so don’t blame them if you can’t sleep.
3) Most cicadas have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.

Panda

Interesting Facts About Panda

1) Giant pandas (often referred to as simply “pandas”) are black and white bears. In the wild, they are found in thick bamboo forests, high up in the mountains of central China .
2) Pandas are BIG eaters – every day they fill their tummies for up to 12 hours, shifting up to 12 kilograms of bamboo!
3) The giant panda’s scientific name is Ailuropoda melanoleuca, which means “black and white cat-foot”.

Teddy-Bear

Interesting Facts About Teddy-Bear

1) Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
2) The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff.
3) The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
